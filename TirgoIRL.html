Applications in Real Life

  • Architecture & Construction: Used to calculate angles, slopes, and structural load paths in buildings and bridges.
  • Astronomy: Helps in calculating distances to stars and planets using parallax and angles.
  • Navigation: Used by pilots and sailors to find direction, elevation, and distance using triangulation.
  • Engineering: Essential in mechanical, civil, and electrical engineering to design components, analyze forces, and optimize systems.
  • Physics: Helps analyze wave motion, oscillations, and vectors in forces and motion.
  • Music & Sound: Trigonometric functions model sound waves and are used in acoustics and digital sound processing.
  • Game Development: Used in 3D modeling and graphics for movement, rotation, and animation of objects.
